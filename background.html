Background & Problem Definition

Understanding why attendance tracking is broken — and what it costs.

The Context

Attendance tracking is a fundamental administrative task across schools, organizations, workplaces, and institutions of all kinds. Yet despite its importance, the methods used to record attendance have remained largely unchanged for decades.

In most environments, attendance is still taken manually — through roll calls, paper sign-in sheets, or spreadsheet entry. These processes are slow, error-prone, and offer no real-time visibility into who is actually present at any given moment.

For educators, administrators, and event organizers, this creates a constant burden: time is wasted, records are unreliable, and the data needed to make informed decisions simply isn't available when it's needed most.

~15 min Lost per session to manual roll call in a typical classroom
High Rate of data entry errors in paper-based attendance systems
0% Real-time visibility in traditional manual tracking

The Problem

Traditional attendance tracking is broken. It wastes time, creates errors, and leaves you guessing who's actually present.

📋

Manual tracking is inefficient

Time spent calling roll, passing sign-in sheets, or typing names could be spent on what matters.

📝

Paper sign-ins are unreliable

Sheets get lost, damaged, or filled with illegible handwriting. There's no backup, no search, no analytics.

⚠️

Human error is common

Missed names, duplicate entries, and forgotten logs lead to inaccurate records when you need them most.

👁️

Real-time visibility is missing

Without live data, you can't confidently answer "who's here right now?" until it's too late.
